# Career Narrative

## Short Arc

I started from a software engineering base and moved deeper into cybersecurity and AI security.
The progression is consistent: build systems, test them under stress, and improve decision quality through explicit constraints.

## Timeline

### 2025: Datadoit - Cybersecurity and AI Engineer Intern

This phase is central to my current positioning.
I designed and implemented PrompTrend, a continuous vulnerability discovery and risk scoring system for large language models.
I focused on black-box LLM evaluation for jailbreaks, prompt injection, and misuse patterns.
This work became a published research output on arXiv.

### 2024: RedStart - Software Engineer Intern

I worked on cloud-hosted web application security and deployment hardening.
I performed penetration testing, improved controls, and contributed to secure backend architecture and CI/CD practices.

### 2023: Freelance Engineering

I delivered secure web tools that integrated machine learning for real use cases.
One outcome was reducing manual sorting effort by about 50 percent in document-classification workflows.

### 2022: Carrier - Junior Student Intern

I supported simulation and analysis work for system optimization in HVAC contexts.
This reinforced engineering validation discipline and data-informed iteration.

### 2021 to Present: Ongoing Applied Roles

- Apple Developer and Tester: iOS beta validation with attention to stability, privacy, and AI feature behavior.
- SMU Library Officer: process digitization and operational improvements.

### 2020 to 2023: Leadership and Community

- Sponsoring Manager at IEEE SMU Student Branch: team leadership and funding strategy execution.
- Rotaract member: sustained community project participation and coordination.

## What This Trajectory Shows

- I did not pivot randomly between domains.
- I consistently moved toward systems that require both engineering depth and adversarial thinking.
- I value operational credibility: reproducible outputs, explicit controls, and measurable outcomes.
